about myself

Hello! I'm Sebastian Quiambao, better known as Isaac by my colleagues. I am a passionate journalist and artist with a keen eye for news and technical writing, photography, and graphic design. My work is driven by a love for objectivity and creativity, a commitment to serve the public if ever given the chance and with my future clients, and a goal to reach my fullest potential and push the boundaries of my skills and capabilities. I specialize in combining narratives into beautiful layouts and graphics. I use various programs with key my skills in:

  • Office Suite software
  • Production Managing
  • Technical and News Writing
  • Editorial and Feature Writing
  • Graphic Design (Canva & Figma)
  • Video Editing (CapCut & Filmora)
  • Event Organizing
  • Social Media Marketing

journalism

Journalism has been my passion since my high school years. I love documenting people's lives, uncovering the stories behind what I observe, and shedding light on the often-overlooked aspects of everyday life. It has always felt like a calling to amplify the voices of the marginalized and disregarded, no matter how big or small their struggles may be. My goal is to protect and acknowledge the truth that remains unheard and to advocate for equity and equality through storytelling. News, features, columns, or technical writing—no matter the format, as long as I can write, I will write.

creatives

Even though I'm terrible at drawing, my appreciation for art has always driven me to explore various forms of human creativity. Whether it’s singing, dancing, designing, or simply admiring others' work, art keeps me grounded and inspired. I consider myself a "jack-of-all-trades"—someone who loves to experiment with different artistic mediums as a way to express myself and push my creative boundaries. These days, I focus on graphics and layout design to better visualize both my own narratives and those of others—fully capturing the essence of our stories.

photography

Photography is the perfect blend of my passion for journalism and creativity. It allows me to capture not just the visual appeal of a person or place, but also the story behind the image. Whether it's a person sitting on the ground listening to music, children playing in the slums, or historical monuments standing the test of time, I always take a picture—freezing a moment to cherish and revealing what we often overlook in our busy lives. Photography, to me, is a visual narrative of time, portraying both the beauty and struggles of a single fleeting moment.
